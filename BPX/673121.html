Brief

Requesting funding, personnel, and test subjects for research and development of an intellectually enhanced, psionically controlled mercenary force.

Intro

Background

A longstanding challenge for GYC has been that of mercenary operations. Despite being one of our most demanded products, mercenary aid is consistently our least profitable; 57% of mercenary operations yield returns below 7%, with an additional 26% of operations resulting in a net loss. There are numerous causes here, but a key issue is that of soldier supply.

An ideal soldier is physically strong, combatively skilled, independently intelligent, and cooperative in response to issued orders. For GYC's purposes, the ideal soldier is also cheap to create/acquire and either renewable or available in large numbers. This poses a problem, as a soldier that satisfies the cost and availability criteria is unlikely to possess the aforementioned skills and characteristics.

Overview

The Hildryn Journal, acquired in PMP Contract 000192327, describes a process for enhancing the intelligence of a primitive being, while also rendering the being vulnerable to psionic control. Application of this process to a group of primitive but physically resilient beings creates an ideal military force when paired with psionic control by a capable leader. The potential of this method was demonstrated by the use of an augmented Sorrowsworn army commanded by Kith'raki in the Second Void War.

Replicating this process could allow GYC to create ideal soldiers at a large scale and at relatively low cost. Doing so would greatly increase the effectiveness and profitability of GYC mercenary services and could even create opportunities for independent military action in the future.

Objectives

Augmentation
Determine process for intellectual enhancement of subjects.
Command
Determine method for psionic influence/control of aforementioned subjects.
Production
Determine method of creating and/or acquiring physically durable subjects.

Requested Resources

  • Supply of Illithid larvae (~10,000)
  • Legal guidance on boundaries of existing GYC noninterference contracts
  • Facilities capable of containing dangerous subjects (e.g. Barlguras)
  • ~3 million gold pieces in initial funding
